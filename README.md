# AirSense & BreatheBand  
Portable and wearable system for real-time air quality and environmental monitoring using embedded sensors and custom hardware.

## Overview
AirSense & BreatheBand is a student-built hardware and software project focused on measuring environmental conditions at both stationary and personal levels.

- **AirSense** → Portable monitoring unit for indoor and outdoor deployment  
- **BreatheBand** → Wearable device for continuous personal exposure tracking  

The system collects, logs, and visualizes air quality and atmospheric data for research and awareness.

## Problem
Public air quality data often comes from distant monitoring stations and does not reflect immediate, personal environments. There is a need for accessible, localized environmental sensing.

## System Design

### AirSense (Portable Unit)
- Designed for field and indoor monitoring  
- High-resolution environmental sampling  
- Suitable for classrooms, homes, and community studies  

### BreatheBand (Wearable Device)
- Wrist-worn form factor  
- Continuous personal exposure measurement  
- Lightweight and low-power operation  

## Measurements
- PM1.0  
- PM2.5  
- PM10  
- Temperature  
- Humidity  
- Atmospheric Pressure  
- Gas / VOC levels  

## Hardware
- PMSA003I Particulate Matter Sensor  
- BME680 Environmental Sensor  
- Microcontroller-based embedded platform  
- Custom portable enclosure  
- Wearable device chassis  

## Software
- Embedded firmware for sensor integration  
- Data logging pipeline  
- Environmental data visualization  

## Results
- Multi-day environmental dataset collection  
- Identification of pollution spikes and environmental trends  
- Validation of low-cost distributed sensing approach  

## Applications
- Personal exposure tracking  
- Classroom air monitoring  
- Community environmental studies  
- Educational demonstrations
